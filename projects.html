Detection of Aimbotting in FPS games using ML

This dissertation explores the detection of cheating in first-person shooter (FPS) games through machine learning techniques, focusing on aim-bot detection and mouse dynamics. Various models, including MLPClassifier, Logistic Regression, and Decision Tree Classifier, were evaluated to assess their effectiveness in identifying cheating behavior.
The results demonstrate that the MLPClassifier performs well, achieving accuracy levels around 80%, which aligns with the studyâ€™s goals. While promising, the research highlights the need for further improvements and ethical considerations in data collection methods. Overall, the project aims to enhance anti-cheat systems within FPS games using advanced machine learning approaches.

The Floor is Coffee

Take control of Joe, a spirited coffee bean with a fierce desire to avoid becoming your favourite drink! As the sea of coffee rises around him, Joe must leap from platform to platform, navigating a treacherous landscape that grows increasingly challenging.
Initially crafted for a game jam hosted by Kyojr on Itch.io, The Floor is Coffee combines charming visuals and addictive gameplay, inviting players to test their reflexes and compete for the top spot on the leaderboard (coming soon). Will you help Joe outrun his fate, or will he succumb to the sea of coffee? Jump in and find out!
